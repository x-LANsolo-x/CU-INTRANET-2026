Dear CUians,

It is my absolute pleasure to welcome you to the Co-Curricular Ecosystem of Chandigarh University. Beyond the classrooms and academic rigor, this ecosystem serves as the crucible where your true potential is discovered and shaped.

Our network of 80+ officially recognized student entities — spanning tech clubs, cultural communities, departmental societies, and global professional chapters — has been established to offer you practical, experiential learning opportunities. By joining these entities, you take on roles that build leadership capacity, foster teamwork, and instigate innovations that make a meaningful societal impact.

In the past years, our co-curricular groups have earned prestigious awards on national and international platforms. Whether it is in aerospace design fests, global hackathons, or national cultural meets, our students consistently prove their competence. I urge you to register your interests, find your unique communities, and participate actively.

Wishing you a transformative co-curricular experience at CU.

Warm regards,

Dr. Raviraja N. Seetharam
Vice Chancellor

FAQs

Got Questions?

Find answers to general questions about registrations, co-curricular credit mappings, and club selections.

How do I register for a club or community?
Simply fill out the Interest Registration form on this platform. The individual club coordinators and faculty mentors will review your responses and reach out regarding auditions, induction dates, or direct selections.
Can I join more than one entity?
Yes. We encourage students to select multiple fields to discover their preferences. Many members actively participate in one technical group and one arts-based community.
Are co-curricular credits mapped to my academic records?
Yes. Participation in officially recognized OAA entities is mapped to Chandigarh University's extra-curricular activity point ledger. Points convert to co-curricular credits upon coordinator audit verification.
What are the eligibility criteria for joining?
General clubs and student communities are open to all branches and semesters. Certain advanced tech societies or performance-based arts troupes may hold audition panels to check skills.
Who handles co-curricular operations?
The ecosystem is overseen by the Office of Academic Affairs (OAA). For specific support, you can connect with your entity's Faculty Champion or submit queries to cocurr.csc@cumail.in.
